Regional Economic Outlook for Asia and Pacific (IMF)
Steady Growth amid Diverging Prospects
April 29, 2024

Growth in Asia and the Pacific outperformed expectations in late 2023, reaching 5.0 percent for the year. Inflation has continued to decline, albeit at varying speeds: some economies are still seeing sustained price pressures, while others are facing deflationary risks. 

In 2024, growth is projected to slow modestly to 4.5 percent. Near-term risks are now broadly balanced, as global disinflation and the prospect of monetary easing have increased the likelihood of a soft landing. Spillovers from a deeper property sector correction in China remain an important risk, however, while geoeconomic fragmentation clouds medium-term prospects. Given the diverse inflation landscape, central bank policies need to calibrate policies carefully to domestic needs. Fiscal consolidation should accelerate to contain debt burdens and debt service cost, in order to preserve budgetary space for addressing structural challenges, including population aging and climate change.

There have been some notable shifts in the global markets recently, especially with regard to China. Following growing speculation about potential fiscal stimulus, the Chinese government has now announced plans to boost the economy. While the markets responded positively, sending Chinese stock indices higher, questions remain about whether these measures will be substantial enough to steer the country away from a deflationary scenario.

Meanwhile, the S&P 500 has reached new highs. The recent resurgence of tech stocks, along with cyclicals like materials and industrials, highlights the changing structure of the market. Over the past few weeks, tech has surged back into leadership, while defensive sectors such as real estate, utilities, and healthcare—which were strong earlier—are now underperforming. This underscores how quickly market dynamics can shift based on economic data and investor sentiment.

In my opinion, the dominance of the “Magnificent 7” tech stocks, semiconductors, and copper points to the strength of long-term trends like AI and green energy. However, with a large portion of market gains concentrated in just a few names, there’s reason to be cautious about the sustainability of this rally. Energy and other sectors continue to lag, reflecting broader economic uncertainty.

As the market continues to realign, it’s crucial to remain flexible and diversified across sectors to navigate these changes effectively.
